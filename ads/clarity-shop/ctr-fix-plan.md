# CTR Fix Plan for The Clarity Shop

## Current Diagnosis

The current Meta Ads issue is weak CTR. The ads are getting impressions, which means Meta is delivering them, but they are not earning enough clicks.

That usually means the creative is not stopping the scroll. The image, hook, headline, or first line is not creating enough emotional pull for the right person to tap through.

Do not optimize Shopify checkout first. Checkout improvements matter after people click, but the current bottleneck is before the product page. The immediate priority is to fix the ad image, headline, and first line so the ad earns attention and clicks.

## What to Fix First

1. **Ad image** — Make the visual feel premium, emotionally specific, and easy to understand in the feed.
2. **Hook** — Lead with a short line that names the problem immediately.
3. **First line** — Use the first sentence of primary text to create recognition, not explanation.
4. **Headline** — Keep it clear, direct, and tied to the product outcome: clarity, self-respect, and an exit strategy.

## Decision Rules

Use CTR as the first creative-quality filter before scaling spend.

- **CTR under 0.5%** = kill the ad.
- **CTR 0.8% to 1.5%** = keep testing with new visual or hook variations.
- **CTR over 2%** = winner. Then inspect the product page, offer, and checkout flow.

## Testing Notes

- Test one clear angle per ad.
- Do not judge an ad only by purchases if CTR is weak; first confirm whether the creative can earn clicks.
- Avoid scaling spend until a creative proves it can stop the scroll.
- Once a creative clears 2% CTR, review the product page and checkout to find the next conversion bottleneck.
